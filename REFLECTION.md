# SpendPilot AI — Reflection & Learning

A self-evaluation of the project and lessons learned during the build.

## 🧠 Technical Learnings
- **Next.js 15 Async Params**: This project forced a deep dive into the breaking changes of Next.js 15. Learning to handle `params` and `searchParams` as Promises in both Pages and Route Handlers was a critical takeaway.
- **Designing for Trust**: I learned that in Fintech/Audit tools, transparency is more important than speed. The "Cinematic Analysis" flow taught me how to use UI to communicate complex backend operations.
- **Tailwind 4.0**: Mastering the new CSS-first configuration approach improved my ability to maintain clean, variable-driven design systems.

## 🚀 Future Improvements
- **LLM-Powered Suggestions**: While the rule-based engine is accurate, integrating GPT-4o to analyze custom tool names (outside the `constants.ts`) would make the product infinitely scalable.
- **Browser Extension**: An extension that scrapes "Billing" pages from ChatGPT/Claude to automatically populate the audit would remove all user friction.
- **OAuth Integration**: Adding Google Workspace OAuth to scan for tool invites/seats in Gmail would be the logical "Pro" feature.

## 📈 Impact
SpendPilot AI solves a real pain point for Seed and Series-A startups who are currently "AI-tool rich but capital poor." By focusing on **capital efficiency** rather than just "saving money," the app positions itself as a growth partner rather than just a cost-cutter.
